Refrigerator Issues

Cooling, leaks, and performance questions.

Common causes include dirty condenser coils, a failing evaporator fan, airflow blockage, or sealed system issues. A full diagnostic helps pinpoint the exact failure and prevent repeat problems.

Leaks are often caused by a clogged drain tube, ice buildup in the drain trough, or debris in the drain path. This can cause water to pool and drip when the door is opened.

Some noise is normal, but loud buzzing, rattling, or grinding can indicate a fan motor issue, compressor stress, or a component that needs adjustment or replacement.

Freezer Problems

Temperature swings and frost buildup.

Poor freezing can be caused by airflow restrictions, failing fans, dirty coils, gasket leaks, or thermostat problems. Quick diagnosis helps prevent food spoilage and compressor stress.

Frost buildup is commonly caused by door gasket leaks, frequent door opening, or defrost system failures. Fixing the root cause prevents recurring ice and improves efficiency.

Ice Maker Troubleshooting

Wine Cooler Cooling

Temperature stability and airflow.

Temperature problems can be caused by a faulty evaporator, fan obstruction, or ice buildup. Cleaning debris around the fan and addressing ice can restore normal cooling.
